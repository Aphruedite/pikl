Why I Build

I’m drawn to products that sit where confusion and consequence meet.

Not as an intellectual exercise. As a pattern I understand from the inside and keep returning to in the products I choose to build.

Founder origin

I didn’t choose this problem space. It chose me.

I moved to the U.S. after an arranged marriage, rebuilt my life through graduate school at Carnegie Mellon, and then watched my future collapse around an immigration-related situation that should have been understood, navigated, and prevented earlier.

The information existed. The support system existed. What did not exist was a trusted place to apply the information to my actual situation before it became irreversible.

What that taught me

The problem is not always lack of information.

In high-stakes systems, people often have too much information and too little orientation. They ask the wrong person, move too late, move too fast, or frame the situation badly because nobody helped them think clearly when it mattered.

“People in high-stakes systems don’t need more content. They need help applying information to a real situation before they act on it.”
Why Pikl exists

Pikl is where my life and product work arrive at the same place.

Across analytics, education, compliance, and safety, I kept working on the same design problem: users inside systems that worked in theory, but failed them in practice when the next step was unclear.

Pikl is the most direct version of that problem I have ever built for — and the most personal.

Current MVP
